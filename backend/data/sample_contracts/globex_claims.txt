Globex Insurance — Internal AI Platform Agreement
Contract ID: GX-2026-0891
Effective Date: February 1, 2026

PARTIES
This agreement is entered between Assurant, Inc. ("Provider") and Globex Insurance
("Customer") for the deployment of AI assistance tools supporting claims processing.

SECTION 1 — SERVICE DESCRIPTION
Provider shall deploy AI-powered analysis tools for Globex Insurance's internal
claims processing team. The tools will assist claims analysts in reviewing,
summarizing, and evaluating insurance claims documentation.

SECTION 2 — DATA HANDLING AND PRIVACY
2.1 All claims data is classified as highly sensitive containing PII and PHI
(Protected Health Information).
2.2 Data processing must occur exclusively within US-based data centers.
2.3 Data must not leave US jurisdiction under any circumstances.
2.4 All data transmissions must use TLS 1.3 or higher encryption.
2.5 Conversation logs must be retained for 7 years per regulatory requirements.

SECTION 3 — APPROVED AI PROVIDERS
3.1 Globex Insurance approves the following AI model providers:
    - Anthropic (Claude model family)
    - OpenAI (GPT model family)
    - Google (Gemini model family)
3.2 No restrictions on specific providers beyond the approved list in 3.1.
3.3 Provider may add new providers from the approved list without additional
    authorization.

SECTION 4 — PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Claims analysis accuracy is the primary performance metric, prioritized
above response speed.
4.2 Analysts may wait up to 5 seconds (5000ms) for thorough analytical responses.
4.3 The system should prioritize reasoning quality and depth for complex
claims analysis tasks.
4.4 System uptime SLA: 99.9%

SECTION 5 — COST AND BILLING
5.1 Globex Insurance operates under a premium SLA tier.
5.2 Customer is willing to pay for higher-quality model tiers when warranted
by query complexity and analytical requirements.
5.3 Monthly AI inference budget: $25,000
5.4 Cost optimization is secondary to output quality for this engagement.

SECTION 6 — USE CASE SPECIFICATIONS
6.1 Primary use case: Internal claims analysis assistant
6.2 User base: 200 claims analysts
6.3 Expected volume: approximately 2,000 queries per day
6.4 Estimated query distribution:
    - Simple queries (policy lookups, status checks): 30%
    - Medium queries (claim summaries, coverage analysis): 40%
    - Complex queries (multi-document reasoning, liability assessment): 30%
6.5 The system must handle multi-document analysis spanning up to 50 pages
of claims documentation.
