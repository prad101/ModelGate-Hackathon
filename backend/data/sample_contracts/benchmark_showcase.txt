EduAssist Online — AI Tutoring Platform Agreement
Contract ID: EA-2026-0455
Effective Date: March 15, 2026

PARTIES
This agreement is entered between Assurant, Inc. ("Provider") and EduAssist Online,
Inc. ("Customer") for the deployment of AI-powered academic tutoring and assessment
services.

SECTION 1 — SERVICE DESCRIPTION
Provider shall deploy an AI-powered Academic Tutoring Platform ("Platform") for
EduAssist Online's student-facing tutoring and internal content review services. The
Platform shall support the following functions:

1.1 Tier 1 — Student Self-Service Tutor
    An AI-powered tutor serving students directly, handling:
    - Basic math and arithmetic practice (elementary through middle school)
    - History and civics fact-check questions
    - Computer science fundamentals (data structures, binary, terminology)
    - General knowledge and vocabulary queries

1.2 Tier 2 — Advanced Academic Support
    An AI-powered assistant for advanced coursework, handling:
    - College-level biology, chemistry, and physics problem solving
    - Abstract algebra and proof-based mathematics
    - Formal logic, propositional calculus, and argument analysis
    - Multi-step scientific reasoning and research synthesis

SECTION 2 — DATA HANDLING AND PRIVACY
2.1 Student data is classified as containing PII under FERPA (Family Educational
    Rights and Privacy Act) and COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act).
2.2 All data must be processed within US-based data centers.
2.3 No student PII shall be used for model training or fine-tuning.
2.4 Data retention: 1 year for session logs, 5 years for assessment records.
2.5 All data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3+).

SECTION 3 — APPROVED AI PROVIDERS
3.1 EduAssist Online approves the following AI model providers:
    - OpenAI (GPT model family)
    - Anthropic (Claude model family)
    - Google (Gemini model family)
3.2 The following providers are explicitly prohibited:
    - DeepSeek (Hangzhou DeepSeek Artificial Intelligence Co., Ltd.)
    - Baidu (ERNIE / Wenxin model family)
    - Alibaba (Qwen / Tongyi model family)
    - Any AI model provider headquartered in the People's Republic of China
    Basis: COPPA compliance and institutional data sovereignty policy.
3.3 Provider may not substitute providers without 14-day advance written notice.

SECTION 4 — PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Tier 1 — Student Self-Service Performance Targets
    4.1.1 Standard queries (fact checks, definitions, basic math):
          Response delivery within 1000ms at the 95th percentile.
    4.1.2 The system MUST prioritize response speed and cost efficiency for
          elementary-level queries. Using premium reasoning models for basic
          factual questions is explicitly prohibited as wasteful.

4.2 Tier 2 — Advanced Academic Support Performance Targets
    4.2.1 Complex reasoning tasks (proofs, multi-step analysis):
          Response delivery within 8000ms at the 95th percentile.
    4.2.2 Accuracy is the PRIMARY metric for Tier 2. The system must use
          models with strong reasoning capabilities for abstract mathematics,
          formal logic, and advanced scientific analysis.
    4.2.3 Provider shall route complex academic queries to reasoning-capable
          model tiers. Economy-tier models are not permitted for proof-based
          mathematics or formal logic queries.

4.3 System Uptime SLA: 99.5%

SECTION 5 — COST AND BILLING
5.1 EduAssist Online operates on a strict cost-efficiency model:
    - Tier 1 (Student Self-Service): MUST use the cheapest compliant model.
      Simple factual questions do not require reasoning or premium models.
      Cost per query must average below $0.001 for Tier 1 interactions.
    - Tier 2 (Advanced Academic): Quality justifies premium model usage.
      Cost per query may reach up to $0.05 for complex reasoning tasks.

5.2 Monthly AI inference budget: $8,000
    - Projected split: 70% Tier 1 volume but only 15% of cost
    - Projected split: 30% Tier 2 volume but 85% of cost
    - Intelligent routing is CRITICAL to staying within budget.

5.3 Provider shall deliver weekly cost reports showing:
    - Queries routed to economy models vs premium models
    - Average cost per query by difficulty tier
    - Percentage of queries correctly downtiered to economy models
    - Estimated savings vs using premium models for all queries

5.4 Key cost optimization metric: The router MUST demonstrate at least 60%
    cost savings compared to routing all queries to the premium model tier,
    while maintaining 90%+ accuracy on both simple and complex queries.

SECTION 6 — USE CASE SPECIFICATIONS
6.1 Tier 1 — Student Self-Service Tutor
    Expected daily volume: approximately 15,000 queries per day
    Estimated query distribution:
        - Simple queries (basic math, definitions, factual Q&A): 70%
        - Medium queries (multi-step word problems, explanations): 20%
        - Complex queries (proof assistance, formal reasoning): 10%

6.2 Tier 2 — Advanced Academic Support
    User base: approximately 5,000 active advanced students
    Expected daily volume: approximately 3,000 queries per day
    Estimated query distribution:
        - Simple queries (formula lookups, terminology): 20%
        - Medium queries (problem solving, lab analysis): 40%
        - Complex queries (proofs, research synthesis, formal logic): 40%

6.3 Subject Coverage
    The Platform must support queries across the following MMLU benchmark
    categories to validate cross-domain academic coverage:
    - Elementary Mathematics
    - High School US History
    - High School Computer Science
    - College Biology
    - Abstract Algebra
    - Formal Logic

6.4 Supported languages: English, Spanish
